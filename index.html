Introduction

A Return to Home (RTH) outcome is benefitial in many ways -- it helps keep families together, reduces shelter intake, and strengthens community trust. This tool also highlights the financial implication of an RTH by significantly shortening animalsâ€™ stay in the shelter.

The premise of this tool is that an RTH outcome eliminates both a one-time cost (for example, spay/neuter, microchipping and vaccinations) and a daily cost of care for each day or care saved compared animals with all other outcomes.

The formula used to calculate cost-savings per RTH outcome is the following:

One-time Cost +

LoS Difference
The average length of stay for strays with all other outcomes minus the average length of stay for strays with RTH outcomes
* Daily Cost of Care = Money Saved

Use the fields under the input section to enter the required data. The output section presents the

RTH rate
The number of stray intakes with RTH outcomes out of all stray intakes
, the cost savings for each RTH outcome, and a few ways to show the savings that will result from improving the RTH rate.
